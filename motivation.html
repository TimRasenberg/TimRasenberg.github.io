Theory, What is motivation?

According to Beata Souders, a researcher with a PHD in psychology. Motivation in its simpelest form is a sense of wanting

We want a change in circumstance, emotion etc. And our behaviour and actions change to accomplish that change

There are multiple forms of motivation, but the 2 most important are Extrinsic and Intrinsic

Extrinsic motivation is a form of motivation that doesn't come from ourselves, it usually comes from people with power over you

This can be a teacher, a boss at your job or a police officer for example. They kind of give orders that give you motivation since they hold power over you

Intrinsic motivation comes from you yourself. This can come from various sources, for example if what you're motivated to do aligns with your moral values

Or if you really want to do something because you just enjoy the activity, for example watching a movie or playing a game.

Dan Pink's View on motivation

According to Dan Pink motivation isn't merely driven by financial sources, hence why people choose jobs they like typically obviously.

And while money CAN be a motivator to do work, it doesn't leave the person with that much satisfaction. It's more of a carrot on a stick approach.

He states that when people notice that they are being paid fairly for their work, they will begin to work for intrinsic motivators like the one's discussed before.

But Pink specifies 3 motivator

  1. Autonomy, a sense of self control that people like to experience.
  2. Mastery, pink argues that getting better at a certain skill set can be extremely satisfying
  3. Purpose, working (especially on things you're interested in) can give the person a great sense of purpose
